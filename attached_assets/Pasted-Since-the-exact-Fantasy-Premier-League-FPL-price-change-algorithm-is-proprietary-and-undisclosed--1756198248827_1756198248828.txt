Since the exact Fantasy Premier League (FPL) price change algorithm is proprietary and undisclosed, any formula is a reasoned approximation based on community insights, historical data, and patterns observed from sources like LiveFPL, Fantasy Football Fix, and r/FantasyPL discussions. These suggest that price changes (rises or falls of £0.1m per day, up to £0.3m per gameweek) are primarily driven by Net Transfers In (NTI = transfers in - transfers out), scaled by a player’s absolute ownership (total number of managers selecting the player). Additional factors like ownership percentage, player price, position, and recent performance may adjust thresholds, but NTI relative to ownership is the core driver.
Hypothesized Formula
Based on analysis, a plausible formula for predicting a price change could be:
Price Rise:
A player’s price increases by £0.1m when:
NTI > T_rise * Ownership_Absolute
Price Fall:
A player’s price decreases by £0.1m when:
NTI < -T_fall * Ownership_Absolute
Where:

NTI: Net Transfers In (transfers in - transfers out) over a 24-hour period (typically reset around 1:30 AM UK time).
Ownership_Absolute: Total number of managers owning the player, calculated as (selected_by_percent / 100) * total_managers (e.g., ~11 million total managers in recent seasons).
T_rise: Threshold coefficient for a price rise, estimated at ~0.045–0.06 (4.5–6% of absolute ownership).
T_fall: Threshold coefficient for a price fall, estimated at ~0.035–0.05 (3.5–5% of absolute ownership).

For subsequent changes in the same gameweek (e.g., second or third £0.1m rise/fall), thresholds likely increase (e.g., by 1.5x for the second change, 2x for the third).

Refinements and Caveats

Dynamic Thresholds: Coefficients (T_rise, T_fall) likely vary by:

Price Bracket: Cheaper players (£4m–£6m) may need lower percentages (e.g., 3–4%) due to smaller transfer markets.
Position: Goalkeepers/defenders might have slightly different thresholds than midfielders/forwards.
Ownership Levels: Very low ownership (<1%) may trigger changes with fewer absolute transfers (e.g., 10,000–20,000).


Other Factors:

Recent points, injuries, or fixture difficulty (FDR) indirectly influence NTI by driving transfers.
Sentiment (e.g., hype on X) can spike NTI unexpectedly—monitor via x_keyword_search for player mentions.


Data Validation: Use historical data (e.g., from vaastav/Fantasy-Premier-League GitHub) to test. For instance, analyze NTI before known price changes in 2023/24 to estimate T_rise/T_fall (medians often fall around 4–6% for rises, 3–5% for falls).
Limitations: The formula assumes linear scaling, but FPL may use non-linear or tiered thresholds. Daily API data (transfers_in_event - transfers_out_event) lags slightly, so real-time monitoring via ownership % changes or fan sites is needed.

Implementation Sketch
pythonimport requests
import pandas as pd

# Fetch FPL data
response = requests.get('https://fantasy.premierleague.com/api/bootstrap-static/')
data = response.json()
players = pd.DataFrame(data['elements'])
total_managers = data['total_players']  # e.g., 11,000,000

# Calculate absolute ownership and NTI
players['ownership_abs'] = (players['selected_by_percent'] / 100) * total_managers
players['nti'] = players['transfers_in_event'] - players['transfers_out_event']  # Daily delta

# Predict price change
T_rise, T_fall = 0.05, 0.04
players['predicted_change'] = 0
players.loc[players['nti'] > T_rise * players['ownership_abs'], 'predicted_change'] = 0.1
players.loc[players['nti'] < -T_fall * players['ownership_abs'], 'predicted_change'] = -0.1

print(players[['web_name', 'now_cost', 'nti', 'predicted_change']])
